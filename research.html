Stanford Radiological Sciences Laboratory (Jun 2012 - Aug 2012)

Stanford PhD student Scott Hsieh had come up with a novel way to reduce patient radiation exposure during CT scans: use moving metal wedges to block excess radiation based off of the patient's physical profile. For example, imaging a patient's arm should take less radiation than imaging a patient's torso since the former is thinner. Similarly, imaging a patient's torso from the front should take less radiation than imaging a patient's torso from the side.

So, Scott asked me to build a prototype device that could move metal wedges during the imaging process. Over the course of several weeks and over many iterations (thank the heavens for the awesome turnaround time of 3D printers), I produced a working prototype. Here's a poster outlining my work:
