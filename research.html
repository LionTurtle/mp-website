Stanford Radiological Sciences Laboratory

Built a medical device prototype to support Stanford PhD student Scott Hsieh's research. Scott had come up with a novel way to reduce patient radiation exposure during CT scans: use moving metal wedges to block excess radiation based off of the patient's physical profile. For example, imaging a patient's arm should take less radiation than imaging a patient's torso since the former is thinner. Similarly, imaging a patient's torso from the front should take less radiation than imaging a patient's torso from the side.
